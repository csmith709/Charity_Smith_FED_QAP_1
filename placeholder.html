Eastern Coyote

The eastern coyote is a species that I though was always in Newfoundland. However it seems that the small canine creature is a relatively new addition. Official reports claim that they came over on the ice flows and spread rapidly, covering the province in less than ten years. However this is debated as some believe they were purposely introduced in hopes to diminish the abundance of hare native to the province and even the overpulated moose that have thrived with their only main predator being humans.

The research for this project has changed my perspective on the animal. Not only for it's newness, but also for it's DNA. The eastern coyote is one of 8 cooyote varieties and is a cross between coyotes, wolves, and dogs. It is a breed of coyotes that is not even full coyote. They are often known to mate with dogs and have more recently been accociated with mating with wolves which introducted the coyote hybrid.

Newfoundland is the only place known to have white coyotes. These are not albino cyotes - whcih have been found elsewhere, these are bonified white. Some scientists believe that they bay be due to a cross between the eastern coyote and a golden retiever, but no testing has been done so it is merely speculation.
